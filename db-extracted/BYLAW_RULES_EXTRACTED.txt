VI. ATTENDANCE AND PARTICIPATION
================================================================================

16. ATTENDANCE REQUIREMENTS (Article 14)
    Category: Attendance Policy
    
    Rule Name: Class Attendance Requirement
    Original Text: "Study in the program is based on regular system with no absence allowed. 
    Student attendance is monitored following conditions set by Faculty Council. 
    A student's attendance rate must be at least 42% to enter final exam."
    Numeric Values:
    - Minimum attendance requirement: 42%
    - Attendance monitoring: Mandatory
    - Regular participation grades: Assigned per course
    
    Consequences for Absence:
    - More than 25% absence (without excuse): Faculty may prevent exam entry 
      after warning and calculate "Pass by Excuse" grade
    - More than 25% absence (with excuse): Faculty may accept with "Pass by Excuse" grade
    - Complete absence from exam: 
      * Without excuse: "Withdrawn" grade recorded
      * With valid excuse: May take exam within 2 days of excuse submission
      * Exception: May write "Incomplete" if certain conditions met

17. FINAL EXAM REQUIREMENTS (Article 14)
    Category: Examination Requirements
    
    Rule Name: Exam Entry Conditions
    Original Text: "Student must achieve at least 42% attendance in lectures and 
    exercises in each course to enter the final exam. 42% of the course grade is required."
    Numeric Values:
    - Attendance minimum: 42% of lectures
    - Participation minimum: 42% of course components
    - Minimum final grade for entry: At least 10% of seasonal work grades to take exam
    
    Conditions:
    - Exam administered within university campus
    - Electronic exams may be conducted
    - Partial electronic grading allowed if approved
    - Display to councils required for approval (Student Affairs, Education, University)

18. GRADING SCALE AND GRADE POINTS (Article 17)
    Category: Grading System
    
    Rule Name: Course Grade Distribution
    Original Text: "Distribution of exam grades per course follows pattern system as 
    specified in table based on course characteristics..."
    Numeric Values:
    
    Grade Distribution (Course Score 100%):
    - Seasonal work (semester work): 40% (based on pattern table)
      * Mid-semester exam: Up to 47% of course grade
      * Course work and tests: 10%
      * Other exams: 10%
      * Practical applications: 40%
    - Final exam: 14% of end of semester exam
    
    Grade Point Scale:
    - A+: 4.0 (96% and above)
    - A: 3.7 (92-95%)
    - A-: 3.4 (88-91%)
    - B+: 3.2 (84-87%)
    - B: 3.0 (80-83%)
    - B-: 2.8 (76-79%)
    - C+: 2.6 (72-75%)
    - C: 2.4 (68-71%)
    - C-: 2.2 (64-67%)
    - D+: 2.0 (60-63%)
    - D: 1.5 (55-59%)
    - D-: 1.0 (50-54%)
    - F (Fail): 0 (Below 50%)
    - Abs (Absent from final without excuse): 0

19. COURSE GRADES WITHOUT CREDIT HOURS (Article 19)
    Category: Special Grading Codes
    
    Rule Name: Non-Credit Grading System
    Original Text: "For Pass/Fail courses without credit hours, grades are recorded as follows..."
    Numeric Values: N/A
    
    Special Grade Codes:
    - P (Pass): Successful completion
    - F (Fail): Course failure
    - W (Withdrawn): Course withdrawal with/without exception
    - Abs (Absent): Absence from final exam without Faculty Council excuse
    - I (Incomplete): If course had year-long work or failed to complete
    - Con (Continuing): Course continuing into next semester without credits
    - W (Withdrawn): Whether pass or fail status

20. MINIMUM PASSING GRADE (Article 16)

    Numeric Values:
    - Minimum course grade: 40% of total
    - Minimum final exam: 30% of final exam score

VIII. GPA AND CUMULATIVE GRADE CALCULATIONS
================================================================================

21. GPA CALCULATION (Article 18)
    Category: GPA System
    
    Rule Name: Semester Grade Point Average (GPA)
    Original Text: "GPA is the average of grade points earned by student for single 
    semester, calculated from grade points only."
    Numeric Values:
    - Scale: 0-4.0
    - Decimal precision: Up to three decimal places
    - Includes: Only one completed semester
    - Calculation method: Average of pattern rates
    
    Transcript shows:
    - Earned grade points
    - Percentage of earned points  
    - Overall GPA by semester

22. CUMULATIVE GPA (CGPA) (Article 18)
    Category: Cumulative GPA System
    
    Rule Name: Overall Cumulative Grade Point Average
    Original Text: "CGPA is average grade points earned by student across all 
    study semesters, extending to three decimal places, showing earned pattern rates, 
    percentage of earned points, and cumulative overall GPA for graduation."
    Numeric Values:
    - Scale: 0-4.0
    - Decimal precision: Up to three decimal places (e.g., x.xxx)
    - Calculation: Average across all completed semesters
    - Uses: Determines academic standing and honors

23. GENERAL GPA FOR GRADUATION (Article 20)
    Category: Academic Standing
    
    Rule Name: Overall GPA Classification at Graduation
    Original Text: "Upon graduation, student transcript shows overall GPA classification..."
    Numeric Values:
    
    GPA Classifications:
    - Poor: Below 1.0
    - Weak: 1.0-2.0
    - Satisfactory (Minimum Pass): 2.0-2.5
    - Good: 2.5-3.0
    - Very Good: 3.0-3.5
    - Excellent: 3.5 and above

IX. ACADEMIC WARNING AND DISMISSAL
================================================================================

24. ACADEMIC WARNING (Article 25)
    Category: Academic Probation
    
    Rule Name: Academic Probation Status
    Original Text: "Student receives academic warning if cumulative GPA in any regular 
    academic semester (excluding first semester) falls below 2.0 and is placed under 
    academic observation."
    Numeric Values:
    - Threshold: CGPA < 2.0
    - Applies to: All regular semesters except first
    - Status: Academic observation placed on student record
    
    Implications:
    - Student academically observed
    - Affected student designation on record
    - Student must improve GPA

25. DISMISSAL FROM FACULTY (Article 26)
    Category: Academic Dismissal Policy
    
    Rule Name: Termination from Faculty Program
    Original Text: "Student dismissed from faculty if meets one of these dismissal conditions:
    1. Academic warning status for 4 consecutive regular semesters or 6 non-consecutive semesters
    2. Exceeds recommended study duration (4 years = 8 regular semesters) at faculty"
    Numeric Values:
    - Consecutive warning semesters: Maximum 4 consecutive
    - Total warning semesters: Maximum 6 non-consecutive
    - Maximum study duration: 4 academic years (8 regular semesters)
    
    Exceptions:
    - May provide additional opportunity
    - Registration allowed in 2 consecutive regular semesters + optional summer semester
    - Must pass minimum 12% of total required credit hours for graduation
    - Requires Faculty Council and University Council approval

26. DISMISSAL FOR DURATION EXCEEDS (Article 26)
    Category: Academic Dismissal
    
    Rule Name: Maximum Study Duration Rule
    Original Text: "Student dismissed if exceeding recommended study period duration 
    with break deductions. This occurs when exceeding 4 academic years and faculty 
    approved break period."
    Numeric Values:
    - Maximum regular semesters without dismissal: 8
    - Additional attempts: May request extension (with conditions)
    - Minimum pass requirement: 12% of total graduation hours
    
    Extension Requirements:
    - Faculty Council approval required
    - University Council approval required
    - At least 12% of required hours completed

X. COURSE WITHDRAWAL AND LEAVE OF ABSENCE
================================================================================

27. COURSE WITHDRAWAL (Article 13)
    Category: Course Withdrawal Policy
    
    Rule Name: Course Drop/Withdrawal Procedure
    Original Text: "After registering for courses, student may withdraw from one or 
    more courses until end of 7th week from start of semester registration as long as 
    registered credit hours don't fall below minimum. Student not considered failing in 
    this case and receives 'W' grade (withdrawn) counted as credit hours."
    Numeric Values:
    - Withdrawal deadline: End of week 7 from registration start
    - Minimum GPA not affected: Withdrawal doesn't count as failing
    - Grade recorded: W (Withdrawn)
    - Credit hours: Still counted in calculation but not for GPA
    
    Excused vs. Non-excused Withdrawal:
    - Without Faculty excuse: "Withdrawn" grade recorded
    - With Faculty excuse (before exam): "Excused Pass" grade  
    - After exam start (with excuse): Grade based on valid excuse
    - Repeated withdrawal exceeds limit: "Withdrawn" grade

28. SUMMER SEMESTER WITHDRAWAL (Article 13)
    Category: Course Withdrawal - Summer
    
    Rule Name: Summer Semester Withdrawal
    Original Text: "For summer semester, student may withdraw from courses after 
    2nd week registration using procedures. Minimum 2 credit hours with maximum 7 
    possible for graduation."
    Numeric Values:
    - Minimum credit hours: 2
    - Maximum possible: 7 for graduation
    - Deadline: After 2nd week of registration start
    
    Withdrawal limits subject to Faculty Council regulations.

29. LEAVE OF ABSENCE (Article 15)
    Category: Academic Leave/Withdrawal
    
    Rule Name: Leave from Study Program
    Original Text: "Student considered withdrawn from study if not registered in 
    academic semester or withdraws from all registered courses without excuse."
    Numeric Values:
    - No limit on approved leaves
    - Consecutive regular semesters leave: Maximum 4 consecutive
    - Total non-consecutive leaves: Maximum 6 regular semesters
    
    Conditions for Approval:
    - Maximum 4 consecutive semesters (or 6 non-consecutive)
    - Faculty Council approval required
    - Without leave: Considered withdrawn
    - Can request deferred status end by week 7 of semester start
    - Faculty Council must approve leave

30. DISMISSED WITH LEAVE APPROVAL (Article 15)
    Category: Dismissal with Consideration
    
    Rule Name: Conditional Leave Status
    Original Text: "Student may take leave (with excuse) for 2 consecutive regular 
    semesters or 4 non-consecutive semesters as maximum baseline. If exceeding 
    approved period without Faculty excuse, dismissed. If with Faculty excuse before 
    exceeding period, receives 'Excused Pass' grade."
    Numeric Values:
    - Approved consecutive leave: 2 semesters maximum
    - Approved non-consecutive leave: 4 semesters maximum
    - Exceeding limit without excuse: Dismissal
    - Exceeding with excuse: May continue under conditions
    - Dismissal remains: "Withdrawn" status in course
    
    Options After Dismissal:
    - May not be dismissed academically
    - Receives "Excused Pass" if Faculty approves excuse before exam
    - Grade status: "Withdrawn" if no excuse documented

XI. COURSE REPETITION AND GRADE IMPROVEMENT
================================================================================

31. COURSE REPETITION AFTER FAILURE (Article 22)
    Category: Course Repetition Policy
    
    Rule Name: Retaking Failed Courses
    Original Text: "If student fails a course, must retake and pass the course. 
    If succeeds after retaking, receives highest grade earned (maximum B = 2.3) 
    calculated once in credit hours and based on new attempt for cumulative GPA."
    Numeric Values:
    - Failed course: Must retake
    - Maximum repeatable grade: B (2.3)
    - Grade recorded: Highest earned attempt
    - Credit hours: Counted only once
    - All attempts appear: On official transcript
    
    Calculation Method:
    - Uses new grade for GPA calculation
    - Only highest grade counts for GPA
    - Credit hours counted once
    - Both original and new grades shown on transcript
    - If student doesn't improve, highest grade still counts

32. COURSE RETAKE FOR GPA IMPROVEMENT (Article 23)
    Category: Voluntary Course Repetition
    
    Rule Name: Voluntary Course Retake for Higher Grade
    Original Text: "If student wants to improve CGPA by retaking previously passed 
    course where successful, receives highest grade earned (no more than B = 2.3) 
    in all retakes. Maximum of 3 courses may be retaken."
    Numeric Values:
    - Maximum retakes for improvement: 3 courses
    - Maximum grade from retakes: B (2.3)
    - Credit hours: Counted once only minimum: CGPA must be below 2.0
    - CGPA threshold for improvement retakes: Below 2.0
    
    Conditions:
    - Course must be same or lower level than enrollment
    - Student must have previously passed course
    - Grades recorded for all attempts
    - Only highest grade counts for CGPA
    - Highest grade shown on official record

33. GRADUATION PROJECT REQUIREMENTS (Article 21)
    Category: Capstone Projects
    
    Rule Name: Project Requirements and Grades
    Original Text: "Only students passing 85 credit hours may register for project. 
    Minimum passing for project: 40% of total project grade and 15% for final review. 
    50% of minimum passing grade for coursework, 15% for oral final defense (via committee)."
    Numeric Values:
    - Prerequisite: Minimum 85 credit hours passed
    - Total credit hours: 6 (3 each for PR411 and PR412)
    - Minimum passing: 40% of project grade
    - Final defense minimum: 15% 
    - Coursework component: 50% minimum for semester work
    - Final defense grade: 15% of total project grade
    
    Components:
    - Project: 6 credit hours
    - Project (1): 3 credit hours (analysis/formulation)
    - Project (2): 3 credit hours (design/implementation)
    - Occurs in consecutive semesters (Fall then Spring)

SUMMARY OF KEY NUMERIC THRESHOLDS
================================================================================

DEGREE REQUIREMENTS:
- Total credit hours: 132
- Minimum cumulative GPA: 2.0
- Maximum study duration: 4 academic years (8 regular semesters)
- Minimum study duration: 3 academic years (6 semesters)

REGISTRATION LIMITS:
- Minimum per semester: 2 credit hours
- Maximum per semester (varies by level):
  * Freshman: 27 credit hours
  * Sophomore: 30 credit hours
  * Junior: 22 credit hours
  * Senior: 132 credit hours (to complete)
- Summer semester minimum: 2 credit hours
- Summer semester maximum: 7 credit hours

ACADEMIC STANDING:
- Minimum passing grade: 40% of course + 30% of final exam
- Minimum GPA threshold: 2.0
- Academic warning threshold: Below 2.0 CGPA
- Honors minimum CGPA: 3.0
- Very Good minimum: 3.0
- Good range: 2.5-3.0

ATTENDANCE:
- Minimum attendance requirement: 42% of lectures
- Absence limit for exam entry: Up to 58% (below 42% prevents exam)
- Excessive absence threshold: 25%

DEADLINES:
- Add/drop deadline: End of week 2 from registration start
- Course withdrawal deadline: End of week 7 from registration start
- Deferred status request deadline: End of week 7 of semester

DISMISSAL CONDITIONS:
- Academic warning consecutive: 4 consecutive semesters maximum
- Academic warning total: 6 non-consecutive semesters maximum
- Duration dismissal: Exceeding 8 regular semesters
- Leave of absence maximum: 4 consecutive or 6 non-consecutive semesters

PROJECTS:
- Graduation project prerequisite: 85+ credit hours passed
- Project duration: 7 weeks per project (1 & 2)
- Project credit hours: 6 total (3 each)
- Minimum project grade: 40% of total, 15% final defense

GRADING SCALE GPA:
- A+ (4.0): 96-100%
- A (3.7): 92-95%
- A- (3.4): 88-91%
- B+ (3.2): 84-87%
- B (3.0): 80-83%
- B- (2.8): 76-79%
- C+ (2.6): 72-75%
- C (2.4): 68-71%
- C- (2.2): 64-67%
- D+ (2.0): 60-63%
- D (1.5): 55-59%
- D- (1.0): 50-54%
- F (0): Below 50%
